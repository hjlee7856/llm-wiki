# Tteokbokki

## Summary

Tteokbokki is a Korean dish of chewy rice cakes simmered in a spicy, slightly sweet sauce. The classic street-food style uses anchovy-kelp stock for depth, then cooks rice cakes with gochujang, gochugaru, sugar, and green onion until the sauce thickens.

## Current Synthesis

- Use cylindrical rice cakes (`garaetteok`), ideally fresh. If frozen, thaw and soak them first.
- Build the base with water, dried anchovies, and dried kelp to create a light stock.
- Mix gochujang, gochugaru, and sugar separately, then add the mixture to the strained stock.
- Simmer the rice cakes with green onion and optional fish cakes or boiled eggs until the rice cakes soften and the sauce turns glossy.
- Serve immediately while hot; the texture is best right after cooking.

## Tensions or Contradictions

- Some modern versions add cheese, ramen, dumplings, or cabbage, but the core recipe does not require them.
- Other tteokbokki variants such as `jeukseok-tteokbokki`, `rosé tteokbokki`, and `gungjung-tteokbokki` follow different cooking logic and should not be treated as the same dish structure.
